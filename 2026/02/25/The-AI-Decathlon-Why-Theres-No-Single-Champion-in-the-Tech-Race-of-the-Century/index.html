Which AI Model Should You Use? (Feb 2026)

发表于 2026-02-26 阅读次数：

Stop asking which AI is “best.” The question is outdated.

The models that matter right now are Gemini 3 Pro, GPT-5.2, and Claude Opus 4.5. They’re all excellent. They’re all different. Pick based on what you need to do.

Why Benchmarks Aren’t The Whole Story

Two things break any benchmark comparison:

Data contamination. If benchmark questions leaked into training data, the model isn’t solving the problem—it’s remembering the answer. You can’t tell if it actually learned.

Teaching to the test. A company can optimize a model to crush one benchmark while being mediocre at everything else. The numbers look great. Real work doesn’t.

Use benchmarks to narrow the field, not to decide. Benchmarks measure controlled environments. You need real performance.

General Reasoning (MMLU-Pro, GPQA)

Gemini 3 Pro leads MMLU-Pro (expert knowledge questions) at 90.1%.

On GPQA Diamond (graduate-level science), GPT-5.2 edges ahead at 94.3%, with Claude Opus 4.5 at 93.8% and Gemini 3.1 Pro at 93.2%. The gap is 0.5%. It doesn’t matter.

All three handle complex reasoning. Pick one based on the next section.

Conversation Quality (Arena-Hard, User Preference)

Arena-Hard measures user preferences across thousands of comparisons. Gemini 3 Pro wins overall at 1492 Elo.

But this shifts by task. Claude Opus 4.5 (Thinking) reaches 1510 in coding and complex problem-solving, slightly ahead of Gemini 3 Pro.

Use Gemini 3 Pro for general conversation. Use Claude 4.5 (Thinking) if you need it to work through complex steps methodically.

Code Work (SWE-bench)

SWE-bench measures how many real GitHub bugs a model can fix autonomously.

GPT-5.2 reaches ~82%. GPT-5 is at 74.9%. The version matters—latest is better.

Claude Opus 4.5 also performs well on SWE-bench Verified, competing closely with GPT-5.2.

For production code fixes, either works. GPT-5.2 has the edge.

Multi-Step Tasks With Tools (GAIA)

GAIA tests complex goals that need research, planning, and tool use—the kind of work that requires the AI to figure out what to do next.

Claude Sonnet 4.5 (not Opus, Sonnet) scores 74.6%. This is the model that developers report can “handle months of architectural work” autonomously.

If you’re delegating entire features or complex system design, use Claude Sonnet 4.5. It’s better at the “what do I do next?” part.

The Real Split

These three models cluster so tightly on academic benchmarks that the numbers don’t tell you which to pick. Here’s what actually matters:

Gemini 3 Pro: Leads on conversation quality and multi-modal reasoning. Best all-around if you don’t care about code.

GPT-5.2: Best for fixing code bugs. If your bottleneck is a bug backlog, use this.

Claude Opus 4.5 (Thinking): Best for complex reasoning with multiple steps. Also best for Sonnet (lighter weight) variant on autonomous multi-step work.

Pick by task, not by benchmark. You’ll get similar results with any of them—the 1-5% differences on benchmarks don’t translate to real-world gaps.

What Changed Since October 2025

The frontier moved forward fast. Gemini 3 Pro released late January. GPT-5.2 released mid-February. Claude Opus 4.5 (Thinking) came with native reasoning capabilities.

All three now use reasoning modes where the model thinks through a problem before answering. This changed the game. The models are more reliable, more careful, and noticeably better on hard problems.

The “best” model changed twice in six weeks. Don’t chase benchmarks. Ship with what works for your task now.

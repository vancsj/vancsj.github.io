How I Get AI to Write Like a Human

发表于 2026-02-21 阅读次数：

I have ideas. I’m bad at writing prose. So I tried asking AI to help.

The results were garbage.

The Problem with AI Writing

AI-generated articles all sound the same. You’ve seen them:

“Let’s dive into the fascinating world of…”
“In this article, we’ll explore…”
“It’s worth noting that…”

Empty phrases. Forced analogies. Hedged opinions. The structure is correct. The content is vacuous.

I generated several articles this way. They looked professional. Read them, and you’d find nothing. No voice. No insight. Just words arranged in blog-shaped patterns.

What I Actually Needed

I don’t need AI to generate ideas. I have those. What I lack is:

  • Turning rough thoughts into readable prose
  • Structuring a narrative without padding
  • Saying things concisely

The mistake was asking AI to create. What I needed was for AI to refine — without replacing my voice with its default tone.

The Anti-AI-Tone Rules

I wrote a rule file. article-writing-rules.md. Before any writing task, I tell the AI to read it.

The rules are simple:

Don’t sound like AI.

AI says “Let’s dive into…” → Cut it.
AI says “It’s important to note…” → Cut it.
AI adds a “wrap-up” section restating everything → Delete it.
AI hedges with “on the other hand” → Pick a side.

Do sound like a human.

Say one thing, move on.
Cut 50% of what you wrote.
Have an actual opinion.
End when you’re done, not when you’ve “wrapped up.”

The Workflow

  1. I write ugly. Bullet points. Fragments. Half-sentences. Voice memos transcribed to text. This is the real content — my ideas in raw form.

  2. AI refines, doesn’t create. It takes my rough draft and improves clarity. It preserves my phrasing. It cuts fluff. It doesn’t add new ideas or “enhance” my voice.

  3. I review. If something sounds wrong, I fix the input, not the output. The problem is usually that I didn’t have a clear thought, not that AI failed to dress it up.

What Changed

Before: I’d prompt “Write an article about X.” AI would generate 1000 words of filler. I’d publish it. Nobody would care.

After: I write my idea in 200 words of ugly prose. AI cleans it up to 300 polished words. I publish it. It sounds like me — just better organized.

The difference: AI stops being the author and becomes the editor.

The Rules File

I keep article-writing-rules.md in my repo. Before any writing task, I say:

“Read article-writing-rules.md and follow those rules. Here’s my draft: [content]”

The file includes:

  • Specific phrases to avoid (“Let’s dive into,” “It’s worth noting”)
  • Principles for good writing (one idea per paragraph, cut ruthlessly)
  • Examples of bad vs. good writing
  • Links to articles I like (for style reference) and articles to avoid (anti-patterns)

This way, the rules persist across sessions. I don’t repeat myself. The AI has a consistent style guide.

Why This Works

AI’s default writing style is a statistical average of all blog posts. That average is bland. It’s designed to be inoffensive, which means it’s forgettable.

When you constrain AI with explicit anti-patterns, you’re pushing it away from the center. You’re saying: don’t do the default thing.

The result isn’t perfect. But it sounds like someone saying something — not a content generator filling space.

That’s the goal.

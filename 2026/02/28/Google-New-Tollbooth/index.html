Google: Building the New Tollbooth

发表于 2026-02-28 更新于 2026-03-01 阅读次数：

This is part of a series on the Magnificent Seven. The framing — four distinct scenarios, seven companies that should not be priced as one story — is laid out in the original memo.

本文是科技七巨头系列的一部分。四种截然不同的终局剧本——完整框架见原始备忘录。

Google Search revenue grew 17% in Q4 2025. Zero-click searches — queries that end without the user clicking any result — rose to roughly 58–65% of all searches over the same period. Both of these things are true simultaneously. Understanding how they can both be true is the entire Google investment question.

谷歌搜索收入在 2025 年第四季度增长了 17%。与此同时，零点击搜索——用户完成查询后不点击任何结果就离开——占到所有搜索的约 58%—65%。这两件事同时为真。理解它们如何能够同时成立，就是整个谷歌投资命题的核心。

The Threat Is Real

威胁是真实的

The original Magnificent Seven memo placed Google in the “Tollbooth Bypass” scenario: the risk is not multiple compression but the elimination of the revenue model itself. The ad auction depends on users clicking links. If AI answers replace that click, the inventory disappears. That risk is not theoretical.

科技七巨头原始备忘录将谷歌归入”收费站被绕开”剧本：风险不是估值倍数压缩，而是收入模式本身的消亡。广告竞价依赖用户点击链接。如果 AI 回答取代了这次点击，广告库存就消失了。这一风险不是理论上的。

AI Overviews appeared in roughly 49% of all Google searches at their 2025 peak. When an AI Overview is present, click-through rates fall to 8% from 15% for traditional results — nearly halved. Users end their search sessions 26% more often when AI answers a query directly versus when they see a traditional results page. Publishers saw a 33% decline in Google-referred traffic between November 2024 and November 2025. Google’s Network advertising revenues — the business of placing ads on third-party websites — declined 1% year-over-year in Q2 2025. The zero-click rate jumping from 56% to 58–65% represents tens of billions of queries where no link-click inventory is generated.

AI Overviews 在 2025 年高峰期出现在约 49% 的谷歌搜索中。当 AI Overviews 出现时，点击率从传统结果的 15% 下降至 8%——几乎减半。当 AI 直接回答查询时，用户结束搜索会话的概率比看到传统结果页时高 26%。出版商在 2024 年 11 月至 2025 年 11 月间谷歌引荐流量下降了 33%。谷歌的网络广告收入——在第三方网站投放广告的业务——在 2025 年第二季度同比下降了 1%。零点击率从 56% 跳升至 58%—65%，意味着数以百亿计的查询再没有产生链接点击库存。

Why Search Revenue Still Grew 17%

为什么搜索收入仍然增长了 17%

The reconciliation is not complicated, but it requires separating three things: query volume, monetization rate per query, and total revenue.

这个看似矛盾的结果并不难理解，但需要区分三件事：查询量、每次查询的货币化率，以及总收入。

Query volume is growing. Total Google search volume grew more than 20% in 2024 and continued expanding in 2025, driven by a larger global user base and AI features drawing more use cases into search. Google reported AI Mode reached 100 million monthly active users in its first two months. The pool is getting bigger even as the click rate per query shrinks.

查询量在增长。受全球用户规模扩大和 AI 功能吸引更多使用场景的驱动，谷歌搜索总量在 2024 年增长超过 20%，并在 2025 年继续扩张。谷歌报告称 AI Mode 在上线两个月内就达到了 1 亿月活用户。这个池子还在变大，即便每次查询的点击率在收缩。

Monetization per query is also holding, for now. Google claims AI Overviews monetize at the same rate as traditional search. The mechanism: ads are embedded directly within AI Overview responses, not only in the sidebar. Google also reports that queries leading to AI Overviews tend to be higher-intent — users who want an AI-summarized answer are often closer to a purchase decision than casual browsers. Whether this holds as zero-click rates continue rising is the open question.

目前每次查询的货币化率也在维持。谷歌声称 AI Overviews 的货币化率与传统搜索相同。其机制是：广告直接嵌入 AI Overviews 回答中，而不仅仅在侧栏。谷歌还报告称，触发 AI Overviews 的查询往往具有更高的购买意图——想要 AI 摘要答案的用户通常比随意浏览者更接近购买决策。但随着零点击率持续上升，这一机制能否持续，是开放的问题。

The current equilibrium: more queries × same monetization per query = growing revenue. The structural concern: as AI answers get better and zero-click rates rise further, the monetization-per-query claim becomes harder to sustain.

目前的均衡：更多查询量 × 相同的单次查询货币化率 = 收入增长。结构性担忧：随着 AI 回答质量提升、零点击率进一步上升，”单次查询货币化率不变”的论断将越来越难以维系。

The Counter-Attack: Gemini and Cloud

反攻：Gemini 与 Cloud

Google is not passively watching its search model erode. The counter-attack is running on two parallel tracks.

谷歌并没有被动地看着自己的搜索模式被侵蚀，反攻正在两条平行轨道上推进。

Gemini’s market share in AI chatbots went from 5.4% in January 2025 to 18.2% in January 2026 — the fastest share gain of any major AI platform in that period. ChatGPT dropped from 87.2% to 68% over the same twelve months. Gemini now has 750 million monthly active users and 8 million paid enterprise seats. The enterprise subscription traction matters: Gemini Enterprise launched in October 2025; the 8 million seat figure was reported at the January 2026 earnings call — roughly three months after launch.

在 AI 聊天机器人市场，Gemini 的份额从 2025 年 1 月的 5.4% 增长至 2026 年 1 月的 18.2%——是同期主要 AI 平台中增速最快的。ChatGPT 在同一年间从 87.2% 下跌至 68%。Gemini 目前拥有 7.5 亿月活用户和 800 万付费企业席位。企业席位才是更有分量的信号：Gemini Enterprise 2025 年 10 月上线，三个月后的财报电话会披露了 800 万这个数字。

Google Cloud is the more decisive number. Q4 2025: $17.7 billion revenue, up 48% year-over-year. Operating margin expanded from 17.5% to 30.1% in a single year — the margin roughly doubled. At an annual run rate now above $70 billion, GCP holds roughly 13% of the global cloud market, behind AWS at 30% and Azure at 20%, but growing at 48% versus AWS’s 17.5%. The gap is closing. The more significant forward signal is the backlog: Google Cloud ended Q4 2025 with $240 billion in contracted future revenue, up 55% from the prior quarter and more than doubled year-over-year. That is not growth optimism — it is signed contracts that have not yet appeared on the income statement.

谷歌云是更决定性的数字。2025 年第四季度：177 亿美元收入，同比增长 48%。营业利润率在一年内从 17.5% 扩大到 30.1%——利润率几乎翻倍。以超过 700 亿美元的年化收入，GCP 占据全球云计算市场约 13% 的份额，落后于 AWS 的 30% 和 Azure 的 20%，但增速为 48%，而 AWS 只有 17.5%——差距在收窄。更重要的前瞻信号是订单积压：谷歌云在 2025 年第四季度末持有 2400 亿美元的合同未来收入，较上一季度增长 55%，较去年同期翻倍。这不是增长乐观情绪，而是尚未出现在损益表上的签约合同。

What is driving the 48% growth? Google does not break out AI versus traditional workloads in its Cloud revenue — unlike Microsoft, which disclosed that AI contributed 16 percentage points of Azure’s 39% growth. What Alphabet did disclose: Q4 2025 revenue from products built on generative AI models grew nearly 400% year-over-year; over 70% of existing GCP customers now use AI products; 85,000 enterprises are building directly on Gemini, 35x more than a year earlier; and the $240 billion backlog is explicitly attributed to AI demand. The directional read is clear — AI is the primary accelerant. But without a percentage-point attribution comparable to Microsoft’s disclosure, the precise share of the 48% that is AI-driven versus traditional migration remains opaque. The risk framing holds regardless: GCP’s growth acceleration is tied to the AI investment cycle. If AI CapEx slows industry-wide, Google’s 48% rate is more exposed than AWS’s 17.5%, whose workload base is more diversified across mature enterprise contracts that predate the current AI spending wave.

48% 的增速来自哪里？谷歌没有在云收入中单独披露 AI 与传统工作负载的构成——不像微软明确披露了 AI 贡献了 Azure 39% 增速中的 16 个百分点。Alphabet 披露的是：2025 年第四季度基于生成式 AI 模型构建的产品收入同比增长近 400%；超过 70% 的现有 GCP 客户目前已在使用 AI 产品；直接在 Gemini 上构建的企业达 8.5 万家，较一年前增长 35 倍；2400 亿美元的积压订单被明确归因于 AI 需求。方向性判断是清晰的——AI 是主要加速器。但在缺乏类似微软那种百分点级归因数据的情况下，48% 增速中有多少来自 AI、多少来自传统迁移，目前是不透明的。风险框架无论如何成立：GCP 的增速加速与 AI 投资周期绑定。如果行业 AI 资本支出整体放缓，谷歌的 48% 增速比 AWS 的 17.5% 更脆弱——AWS 的工作负载基础在 AI 投资潮之前就已建立，由更分散的成熟企业合同支撑。

The strategic read: Google is building a new tollbooth alongside the original one. If Gemini captures the enterprise AI assistant layer and Cloud continues at this margin expansion rate, the revenue model shift is less a cliff edge and more a transition — painful for search’s contribution margin, but offset from above. The question is timing: the $240 billion backlog converts over multi-year contracts while zero-click erosion compounds every quarter.

战略解读：谷歌正在原有收费站旁边建设新的收费站。如果 Gemini 占据企业 AI 助手层，云端以这种利润率扩张速度持续增长，收入模式的转变就更像是一次过渡而非悬崖——对搜索贡献利润率造成压力，但从上方得到补偿。问题在于时机：2400 亿美元的积压订单要通过多年合同逐步转化，而零点击侵蚀每季度都在复利积累。

The Silicon Advantage

硅层优势

The least-discussed part of Google Cloud’s cost structure is its chip program. Google has been building custom AI silicon since 2015 — a decade before this became a strategic imperative for everyone else. The seventh-generation TPU, Ironwood, was deployed in late 2025: 4,614 FP8 TFLOPS per chip, 192GB of HBM3E memory, 7.37 TB/s bandwidth, scaling to pods of 9,216 chips linked by Google’s proprietary interconnect — 42.5 ExaFLOPS per pod. Google claims Ironwood training and inference pods outperform Nvidia GB300 configurations on large model workloads. Whether the benchmark comparisons hold in production is always contested, but the credibility of the claim — verified by Anthropic’s procurement decision — matters: Anthropic, which Google has invested $3 billion in, just signed the largest TPU deal in Google’s history, committing to hundreds of thousands of Trillium chips in 2026 scaling toward one million by 2027. Anthropic’s Claude trains on TPUs, not Nvidia GPUs.

谷歌云成本结构里最少被讨论的部分，是它的芯片项目。谷歌从 2015 年就开始构建定制 AI 芯片——在这成为所有人的战略迫切性之前整整十年。第七代 TPU Ironwood 于 2025 年底部署：单芯片 4,614 FP8 TFLOPS，192GB HBM3E 显存，7.37 TB/s 带宽，通过谷歌自研互联网络扩展到 9,216 芯片的集群——每集群 42.5 ExaFLOPS。谷歌声称 Ironwood 训练和推理集群在大模型工作负载上超越了英伟达 GB300 配置。基准测试在生产环境中是否成立永远有争议，但这个声明的可信度——由 Anthropic 的采购决策背书——很说明问题：谷歌投资了 30 亿美元的 Anthropic 刚刚签下了谷歌历史上最大的 TPU 合同，承诺在 2026 年采购数十万颗 Trillium 芯片，并向 2027 年的百万规模推进。Anthropic 的 Claude 在 TPU 上训练，不是英伟达 GPU。

This matters for two reasons. First, Google’s infrastructure margin expansion — operating margins in Cloud went from 17.5% to 30.1% in one year — is partly driven by the cost efficiency of running its own silicon rather than paying Nvidia’s data center pricing. As TPU deployment scales, the structural cost advantage compounds. Second, Google’s $600 billion AI capex race is partly internal spending: money paid to Nvidia leaves the ecosystem, but money spent on TPU fabs is an investment in Google’s own margin structure. The comparison to Microsoft and Amazon is instructive — both are further from parity on custom silicon, meaning their Cloud margins are more exposed to Nvidia pricing power in the near term.

这在两个层面上很重要。第一，谷歌云的基础设施利润率扩张——营业利润率一年从 17.5% 升至 30.1%——部分来源于自研芯片的成本效率，而不是向英伟达支付数据中心定价。随着 TPU 部署规模扩大，这种结构性成本优势会持续复利。第二，谷歌参与的 6000 亿美元 AI 资本支出竞赛，有相当部分是内部支出：付给英伟达的钱流出了生态，但花在 TPU 晶圆厂上的钱是对谷歌自身利润结构的投资。与微软和亚马逊的对比说明问题——两者在定制芯片上的成熟度落后于谷歌，意味着近期它们的云利润率对英伟达的定价权更为暴露。

The Engagement Gap

参与度差距

Gemini’s market share numbers look strong. The engagement numbers are more honest. ChatGPT users visit the platform 11.52 times per month on average; Gemini users visit 4.2 times. Average session duration: ChatGPT 6 minutes 47 seconds, Gemini 4 minutes 43 seconds. Gemini’s user base is large partly because Google embeds it across Android, Chrome, Workspace, and Search — distribution advantages that inflate monthly active users without necessarily reflecting deep product preference. The users are there; the habit formation is not yet equivalent.

Gemini 的市场份额数字看起来强劲。参与度数字更诚实。ChatGPT 用户平均每月访问平台 11.52 次，Gemini 用户是 4.2 次。平均会话时长：ChatGPT 6 分 47 秒，Gemini 4 分 43 秒。Gemini 的用户规模大，部分原因是谷歌将其嵌入 Android、Chrome、Workspace 和搜索——这些分发优势会拉高月活用户数，但不一定反映深度产品偏好。用户的量到了，但每天回来的理由还没有建立起来。

This is not fatal. Distribution advantages are real advantages — they are how Google built search dominance in the first place. But it means the 750 million monthly users figure overstates Gemini’s current stickiness. The enterprise seats metric is more meaningful: an enterprise buying Gemini seats has made a procurement decision, not just clicked a pre-installed icon.

这不是致命问题。分发优势是真实的优势——这正是谷歌当年建立搜索霸权的方式。但这意味着 7.5 亿月活用户的数字夸大了 Gemini 当前的粘性。企业席位指标更有意义：一个购买 Gemini 席位的企业做出了采购决策，而不仅仅是点击了一个预装图标。

The Antitrust Outcome

反垄断结果

The September 2025 antitrust ruling was better than feared for Google. Judge Mehta barred exclusive distribution deals — Google can no longer contractually lock in default search placement — but explicitly allowed Google to continue paying for non-exclusive default placement. Chrome and Android were not forced to be divested. The remedy is a data-sharing requirement: Google must share certain search index and user interaction data with competitors for six years, monitored by a technical oversight committee.

2025 年 9 月的反垄断裁决对谷歌来说好于预期。法官梅赫塔禁止了排他性分发协议——谷歌不能再通过合同锁定默认搜索位置——但明确允许谷歌继续为非排他性默认位置付费。Chrome 和 Android 未被强制剥离。救济措施是数据共享要求：谷歌必须在六年内与竞争对手共享特定的搜索索引和用户交互数据，并由技术监督委员会监督。

The practical implication: Google still pays Apple for default placement on iPhone; Apple still accepts. The distribution moat survives, weakened but intact. The data sharing order is the more consequential remedy — giving competitors access to Google’s search interaction data could meaningfully accelerate rival search engines’ training pipelines. The case will likely appeal to the Supreme Court; final resolution is not expected before 2027–2028.

实际影响：谷歌仍在为 iPhone 上的默认位置向苹果付费，苹果仍在接受。分发护城河得以保留，有所削弱但仍然完好。数据共享命令是更重要的救济措施——让竞争对手访问谷歌的搜索交互数据，可能会大幅加速竞争对手搜索引擎的训练流程。该案可能上诉至最高法院，最终裁决预计最早在 2027—2028 年。

The Cisco Comparison

思科的类比

The original memo used Cisco as the terminal case: not bankruptcy, but permanent impairment of the core value proposition. Cisco dominated routing infrastructure at the peak of the dot-com era, then watched as the internet’s commodity layer eroded its pricing power. Revenue didn’t collapse — it plateaued, then grew slowly. The multiple never recovered.

原始备忘录使用思科作为终局案例：不是破产，而是核心价值主张遭受永久性损伤。思科在互联网泡沫顶峰主导了路由基础设施，随后看着互联网商品化一刀一刀割掉了它的定价权。收入没有崩溃——停滞了，然后缓慢增长。估值倍数再也没有恢复。

The Cisco comparison applies to Google only if Gemini fails to become the dominant AI layer and search monetization degrades faster than cloud and subscription revenue grows. If Gemini wins — or even holds second place — and Google Cloud continues at 48% growth, the Cisco trajectory doesn’t apply. Google is not a one-product company defending a single revenue stream. It is simultaneously the incumbent in search, a fast-growing cloud platform, and the owner of YouTube’s $11.4 billion quarterly ad business. The Cisco comparison is the right risk to model. It is not the inevitable outcome.

思科类比适用于谷歌的条件：Gemini 未能成为主导 AI 层，且搜索货币化的退化速度快于云端和订阅收入的增长。如果 Gemini 获胜——或者仅仅保持第二位——而谷歌云继续以 48% 的速度增长，思科轨迹就不适用。谷歌不是一家捍卫单一收入流的单产品公司。它同时是搜索领域的在位者、一个高速增长的云平台，以及 YouTube 每季度 114 亿美元广告业务的所有者。思科类比是值得建模的正确风险，不是不可避免的结果。

The Conclusion

结论

The investment question for Google is not whether AI disrupts search. It already is. The question is the rate of substitution versus the rate of replacement revenue growth. Search revenue grew 17% in Q4 2025 even as the structural damage accumulated underneath. Gemini’s enterprise traction is real. Cloud is growing at 48%. The antitrust outcome was survivable. The timing question — when does the zero-click erosion outrun the Gemini and Cloud counter-attack? — remains genuinely open. At 27x forward earnings, the market is pricing in significant execution risk. That pricing is defensible. It is also potentially too punishing if Google holds the AI platform layer.

谷歌的投资命题不在于 AI 是否颠覆搜索，它已经在颠覆了。问题在于替代速度与替代收入增长速度之间的赛跑。2025 年第四季度搜索收入增长了 17%，尽管底层结构性损伤在积累。Gemini 的企业端在走，云端增长 48%，反垄断的结果没有打断节奏。时机问题——零点击侵蚀何时会超过 Gemini 和云端的反攻？——至今没有定论。以 27 倍远期市盈率，市场已经定价了相当大的执行风险。这个定价是合理的，但如果谷歌守住了 AI 平台层，这种定价也可能惩罚过重。

Nvidia: Two Clocks

发表于 2026-02-28 更新于 2026-03-01 阅读次数：

This is part of a series on the Magnificent Seven. The framing — four distinct scenarios, seven companies that should not be priced as one story — is laid out in the original memo.

本文是科技七巨头系列的一部分。四种截然不同的终局剧本——完整框架见原始备忘录。

Nvidia just reported Q4 FY2026: $68.1 billion in total revenue, $62.3 billion from data centers, up 75% year-over-year. Q1 FY2027 guidance is $78 billion — $5 billion above analyst consensus. The stock barely moved. At a trailing PE of ~48x and forward PE of ~30x, the market has two concerns running simultaneously that it treats as one. They are not one. They operate on entirely different timescales, have different triggers, and require different hedges.

英伟达刚刚公布了 FY2026 第四季度业绩：总收入 681 亿美元，数据中心收入 623 亿美元，同比增长 75%。FY2027 第一季度指引 780 亿美元——高出分析师共识 50 亿。股价几乎没动。以约 48 倍的追踪市盈率和约 30 倍的远期市盈率，市场同时怀揣着两种担忧，却把它们当成一件事来定价。它们不是一件事，运行在完全不同的时间尺度上，有不同的触发机制，需要不同的对冲策略。

Clock 1: The CapEx Snap

第一个钟：资本支出的急刹车

Hyperscalers — Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon — account for just over 50% of Nvidia’s data center revenue. Combined, they are spending $600 billion in capex in 2026. The problem is what they are buying it with. AI services revenue across the industry is still a fraction of that spend — the Magnificent Seven memo estimated the ROI gap at roughly $25 billion in AI-attributable revenue against a multiple of that in infrastructure cost. The hyperscalers are funding the CapEx cycle with legacy cash flows, not AI returns.

超大规模云厂商——微软、谷歌、Meta、亚马逊——占英伟达数据中心收入的 50% 出头。合计 2026 年资本支出 6000 亿美元。问题在于这钱从哪来。全行业的 AI 服务收入仍然是这笔支出的零头——七巨头备忘录估算，AI 可归因收入约 250 亿美元，而基础设施成本是这个数字的数倍。超大规模云厂商是在用传统业务的现金流养这轮资本支出周期，不是用 AI 的回报。

This is a classic prisoner’s dilemma. No hyperscaler can stop buying without ceding the AI narrative. The moment any one of them cuts CapEx guidance — even slightly — the forward revenue models for Nvidia compress violently. It does not require the business to break. Nvidia’s data center revenue can be entirely intact while the multiple rerate is brutal: the valuation was built on a growth assumption that no longer holds. That is the textbook Nifty Fifty ending. The underlying company is fine. The multiple was not priced for a plateau.

这是经典的囚徒困境。没有哪家超大规模云厂商敢率先停止购买，否则就是在 AI 叙事中主动掉队。一旦其中任何一家小幅下调资本支出指引——哪怕一点——英伟达的远期收入模型就会遭遇暴力压缩。不需要业务本身出问题。数据中心收入可以原封不动，估值倍数的重估就已经很惨烈：这个估值建立在一个不再成立的增长假设上。这就是教科书里"漂亮 50"的结局——底层公司没问题，但估值从来没有为停滞定价。

The Q1 FY2027 guide of $78 billion — beating consensus by $5 billion — means this snap has not triggered yet. Blackwell demand is real. The Anthropic TPU deal, the AMD MI300X deployments, the custom ASIC progress at every hyperscaler — none of that has been enough to dent Nvidia’s backlog. For now. The clock is running, not stopped.

FY2027 第一季度 780 亿美元的指引——高出共识 50 亿——说明这个急刹车还没有触发。Blackwell 的需求是真实的。Anthropic 的 TPU 大单、AMD MI300X 的大规模部署、每家超大规模云厂商的定制芯片进展——这些都还不足以对英伟达的订单积压造成缺口。目前如此。第一个钟在走，没有停。

Clock 2: The Middleware Erosion

第二个钟：中间件的侵蚀

The second risk has a longer fuse. CUDA is the deepest software moat in enterprise computing — six million developers, 300+ libraries, 600+ validated AI models, a decade of optimization that ROCm has not matched. Running the same workload on AMD hardware using ROCm still costs 10–30% in performance, and in some benchmarks the gap is wider. That moat is real.

第二个风险的引线更长。CUDA 是企业计算里最深的软件护城河——600 万开发者、300 多个库、600 多个经过验证的 AI 模型、十年的优化积累，这些是 ROCm 至今没有追平的。用 ROCm 在 AMD 硬件上跑同样的工作负载，仍有 10%–30% 的性能损耗，在某些基准测试上差距更大。这条护城河不是幻觉。

What erodes a moat is not a competitor matching it head-on. It is abstraction. Triton — now the kernel backend for PyTorch’s TorchInductor, used by vLLM, Mamba, and DeepSpeed — lets engineers write GPU kernels in Python that compile to hardware-specific code via MLIR. The gap between Triton and hand-optimized CUDA is roughly 15–20% on Nvidia hardware. It is closing, not widening. More importantly, Triton kernels run on AMD hardware too. Write once, run anywhere is not a claim — it is the current trajectory. Meanwhile, hyperscaler custom ASICs are taking training workloads that CUDA once owned: Google’s Ironwood TPU (7th generation), Amazon Trainium 3 (4x faster than its predecessor), Anthropic committing to hundreds of thousands of Trillium TPUs. Custom chips accounted for 37% of the AI accelerator market in 2024, 40% in 2025, and are projected at 45% by 2028.

侵蚀护城河的不是正面硬刚。是抽象层。Triton——现在是 PyTorch TorchInductor 的内核后端，被 vLLM、Mamba 和 DeepSpeed 采用——让工程师用 Python 写 GPU 内核，通过 MLIR 编译成硬件专用代码。Triton 和手写优化 CUDA 在英伟达硬件上的差距约为 15%–20%，而且这个差距在收窄，不是在扩大。更关键的是，Triton 内核同样能跑在 AMD 硬件上——"写一次，到处跑"不是营销说辞，而是当前的技术轨迹。与此同时，超大规模云厂商的定制芯片在蚕食 CUDA 曾经独占的训练工作负载：谷歌第七代 Ironwood TPU、亚马逊 Trainium 3（比上代快四倍）、Anthropic 承诺采购数十万颗 Trillium TPU。定制芯片 2024 年占 AI 加速器市场 37%，2025 年 40%，预计 2028 年达到 45%。

This is Intel-ification in slow motion. Intel owned the compute layer for a generation. Then x86 became a commodity abstracted by the operating system, then by virtual machines, then by containers. Each layer of abstraction made the underlying hardware less differentiated. Nvidia is not in that position today — CUDA is too sticky, Blackwell’s performance advantages too real. But the trajectory toward compiler-mediated abstraction is established. This clock does not ring in 2026. It rings somewhere in the 2030–2035 range, and only if Triton + MLIR achieve parity and hyperscaler custom silicon reaches the scale needed to give engineers a real choice.

这是慢动作的英特尔化。英特尔统治了一代人的计算层，然后 x86 被操作系统抽象掉，再被虚拟机抽象，再被容器抽象。每一层抽象都让底层硬件的差异化缩水一分。英伟达今天还不在那个位置——CUDA 太粘，Blackwell 的性能优势太真实。但向编译器媒介抽象化的轨迹已经确立。这个钟不会在 2026 年响，大概在 2030–2035 年的某个时间点，而且需要 Triton + MLIR 真正追平，超大规模云厂商的定制硅达到足够规模、给工程师提供真实选择之后。

Why Conflating Them Is the Error

为什么把两者混为一谈是错误

Clock 1 can trigger in a single earnings cycle. The mechanism is straightforward: one hyperscaler reports a Q2 earnings call with a CapEx guidance cut, the revenue models for Nvidia’s forward year compress, the 30x forward multiple becomes a 20x multiple overnight. No CUDA erosion required. No AMD victory required. Just one quarter of softening demand signal from the customers who represent half the revenue.

第一个钟可以在单季财报里触发。机制很直接：某家超大规模云厂商在财报电话会上下调资本支出指引，英伟达远期年度收入模型随之压缩，30 倍远期市盈率一夜变成 20 倍。不需要 CUDA 被侵蚀，不需要 AMD 获胜——只需要占据一半收入的客户发出一个需求放缓的信号。

Clock 2 requires ecosystem migration measured in years. It requires Triton achieving performance parity on Nvidia hardware (not there yet), MLIR-based toolchains becoming the default for new model development (trend in place, not dominant), and custom ASIC training capability reaching the scale where engineers choose TPUs or Trainium over H100s for new workloads (happening at the margin, not at the center). None of those conditions are met in a single quarter. The hedge for Clock 2 is monitoring framework-level adoption metrics — Triton kernel shares in major training runs, XLA vs CUDA backend ratios — not quarterly CapEx data.

第二个钟需要以年为单位的生态迁移。需要 Triton 在英伟达硬件上实现性能追平（还没到）；需要基于 MLIR 的工具链成为新模型开发的默认选项（趋势在，还不是主流）；需要定制芯片的训练能力达到足够规模，让工程师在新工作负载上主动选择 TPU 或 Trainium 而不是 H100（边缘在发生，不在中心）。这些条件没有一个能在单季度内完成。对冲第二个钟的方式是盯框架层面的采用率指标——Triton 内核在主要训练任务里的占比、XLA vs CUDA 后端的比例——不是季度资本支出数据。

The market is treating both risks as already partially priced into a ~30x forward multiple. The honest answer is that Clock 1 is partially priced and Clock 2 is not. A 30x multiple on a business with 50% customer concentration in a CapEx cycle that is still building is not cheap. It is priced for the cycle to continue. When it doesn’t — not if — the multiple compresses. Clock 2 is so far out that it is not the relevant investment thesis for the next three years.

市场把两种风险都当作已经部分计入约 30 倍的远期市盈率里了。诚实的回答是：第一个钟部分计入了，第二个钟没有。对一个 50% 收入高度集中在仍在扩张的资本支出周期客户身上的业务来说，30 倍市盈率不便宜——它是为周期持续定价的。当周期不再持续——不是如果，而是当——估值倍数会压缩。第二个钟太遥远，不是未来三年的有效投资命题。

The Conclusion

结论

Nvidia’s current business is not broken. Q4 FY2026 delivered $68.1 billion. The Q1 FY2027 guide of $78 billion exceeded every analyst model. Blackwell demand is real, the backlog is real, and the CUDA ecosystem lock-in is real. None of that is in dispute.

英伟达当前的业务没有问题。FY2026 第四季度交出了 681 亿美元。FY2027 第一季度 780 亿美元的指引超出了每一个分析师的模型。Blackwell 的需求是真实的，订单积压是真实的，CUDA 生态锁定是真实的。这些都没有争议。

The risk is valuation, not fundamentals. At ~30x forward earnings, the market is pricing Clock 1 as manageable and Clock 2 as irrelevant. That may be right. But the first sign that Clock 1 is running faster than expected is not a product failure — it is a hyperscaler CapEx guidance cut. Watch the hyperscalers’ quarterly capex lines, not Nvidia’s order book. And for Clock 2, watch Triton adoption in new model training infrastructure, not GPU benchmark comparisons. The two clocks have different indicators. The market conflates them. That is where the mispricing lives — not in Nvidia’s current results, which are exceptional, but in the assumption that what triggers these risks is the same thing.

风险在估值，不在基本面。约 30 倍远期市盈率，市场把第一个钟定价为可控，第二个钟定价为无关紧要。也许是对的。但第一个钟比预期跑得更快的第一个信号，不是产品失败——而是超大规模云厂商的资本支出指引下调。要盯的是超大规模云厂商的季度资本支出行，不是英伟达的订单积压。对于第二个钟，要盯的是 Triton 在新模型训练基础设施里的采用率，不是 GPU 基准测试比较。两个钟有不同的指标。市场把它们混为一谈——这就是定价偏差所在：不在英伟达当前的成绩里（那是出色的），而在于假设触发这两种风险的是同一件事。

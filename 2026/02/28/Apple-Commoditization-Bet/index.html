Apple: The Commoditization Bet

发表于 2026-02-28 更新于 2026-03-01 阅读次数：

This is part of a series on the Magnificent Seven. The framing — four distinct scenarios, seven companies that should not be priced as one story — is laid out in the original memo.

本文是科技七巨头系列的一部分。四种截然不同的终局剧本——完整框架见原始备忘录。

Google, Microsoft, Meta, and Amazon committed a combined ~$380 billion in capex in 2025. Apple spent $12.7 billion. One-tenth of Amazon. Every chart showing this data reads Apple as falling behind. That reading assumes the hyperscalers’ approach is correct. The alternative reading: Apple is the only company not making a bet it might not need to make.

谷歌、微软、Meta 和亚马逊 2025 年合计资本支出约 3800 亿美元。苹果花了 127 亿——仅亚马逊的十分之一。每张呈现这组数据的图表都把苹果读成了掉队者。这种解读的前提是超大规模云厂商的路子是对的。另一种读法是：苹果是唯一一家没有在一场也许根本不需要打的军备竞赛中押注的公司。

The Surface Case Against

表面的反驳

The bear case on Apple AI is concrete. Siri is still bad. The “smarter Siri” upgrade — the capability to take actions inside third-party apps — was promised for spring 2025, delayed to 2026, and has yet to ship. John Giannandrea, who Apple brought from Google to lead AI, has been effectively sidelined in the organizational restructuring. Apple Intelligence launched with iOS 18, but the headline features were writing tools and notification summaries — useful, not transformative. iOS 18 saw below-average adoption despite the Apple Intelligence marketing. Apple’s forward earnings multiple sits at roughly 32x — the most expensive of the Magnificent Seven after Nvidia — priced for an AI product that hasn’t arrived on any timeline Apple originally communicated.

反驳苹果 AI 的理由很具体。Siri 还是很差。”更智能的 Siri”升级——在第三方应用内执行操作的能力——承诺在 2025 年春推出，推迟到 2026 年，至今未交付。苹果从谷歌挖来负责 AI 的 John Giannandrea 在组织架构调整中实际上被边缘化了。Apple Intelligence 随 iOS 18 推出，但主打功能是写作辅助和通知摘要——有用，没有变革性。尽管做了大量 Apple Intelligence 营销，iOS 18 的采用率依然低于历史平均水平。苹果的远期市盈率约 32 倍——七巨头中除英伟达外最贵——却是为一个没有按照任何原始时间表落地的 AI 产品付的钱。

The Strategic Logic

战略逻辑

Apple’s $12.7 billion capex is not a budget constraint. It is a thesis about what matters in an AI world.

苹果 127 亿美元的资本支出不是预算限制，而是一个关于 AI 时代什么才重要的判断。

The thesis: foundation models will commoditize. Not immediately, but structurally — the same way cloud compute commoditized servers. When that happens, the company that owns the last mile wins: the device, the operating system, the trust relationship with the user, the privacy architecture. Apple is betting on being that last mile. Rather than building frontier models, it is buying capacity from whoever is best at any given moment — OpenAI first, now transitioning to Gemini — wrapping them in Apple’s privacy layer and distributing them to 1.5 billion active devices. The model is a commodity input. The ecosystem is the product.

这个判断是：基础模型会商品化。不是立即，而是结构性地——就像云计算使服务器商品化一样。当这发生时，拥有最后一公里的公司就赢了——设备、操作系统、与用户的信任关系、隐私架构。苹果在押注成为那个最后一公里。与其自建前沿模型，它选择从任何时间点最好的供应商处购买能力——先是 OpenAI，现在过渡到 Gemini——套进苹果的隐私架构，分发给 15 亿台活跃设备。模型是商品化的投入，生态系统才是产品。

Private Cloud Compute is the architecture that makes this coherent. When Apple Intelligence processes a request in the cloud, it runs on custom Apple silicon servers — now being upgraded to M5 chips — where the data is encrypted, not stored, and the server-side code is publicly auditable. No other AI cloud provider makes that claim verifiably. As AI gets more intimate with personal data — health records, messages, financial information — this architecture becomes a regulatory and trust moat, not just a marketing claim. The EU’s AI Act, GDPR enforcement, and state-level US privacy regulation are all moving in directions that make Apple’s architecture increasingly relevant.

Private Cloud Compute 是使这一逻辑自洽的架构。当 Apple Intelligence 在云端处理请求时，它运行在定制的苹果芯片服务器上——目前正在升级至 M5——数据在传输中加密、不被存储，服务器端代码可公开审计。没有其他 AI 云服务商能以可验证的方式做出这样的承诺。随着 AI 与个人数据——健康记录、消息、财务信息——的关系越来越密切，这套架构会成为监管和信任的护城河，而不仅仅是营销说辞。欧盟 AI 法案、GDPR 执法和美国州级隐私法规，都在朝着有利于苹果架构的方向收紧。

The Evidence Against the Thesis

反驳这个判断的证据

The model-as-commodity thesis requires models to actually commoditize. That has not happened yet. GPT-5, Gemini 3.1, and Claude Sonnet 5 are not interchangeable; there are real capability differences that matter for real tasks. If frontier model quality continues to diverge — if the gap between the best model and a commodity model stays wide — then Apple’s strategy of buying commodity-tier capability and wrapping it in UX becomes a persistent disadvantage in the actual AI experience delivered to users. Siri’s failure is partly organizational, but it is also a symptom of not owning the model layer at a moment when model quality defines the product.

模型商品化的判断需要模型真正商品化，而这还没有发生。GPT-5、Gemini 3.1 和 Claude Sonnet 5 不是可互换的；它们在实际任务上有实质性的能力差异。如果前沿模型质量持续分化——最好的模型和商品级模型之间的差距保持较大——那么苹果外购商品级能力、套上 UX 外壳的策略，就会在交付给用户的实际 AI 体验上形成持续劣势。Siri 的失败一部分是组织问题——但不掌控模型层的代价，在模型质量定义产品体验的时刻，就这样显现出来了。

The Middleware Dissolution risk from the original memo applies here. Apple’s hardware premium is built on the value of tight hardware-software integration. As AI capabilities shift from device-level computation to model-level inference — where the value is in the model, not the chip running it — the differentiation premium for Apple Silicon narrows. An iPhone 16 Pro running a commodity model is less differentiated from a Samsung running the same model than an iPhone 6 was from any Android device in 2015.

原始备忘录中”中间件解构”的风险在这里同样适用。苹果的硬件溢价建立在软硬件紧耦合的价值之上。随着 AI 能力从设备端计算转向模型层推理——价值在模型本身而非运行它的芯片——Apple Silicon 的差异化溢价在收窄。一台运行商品级模型的 iPhone 16 Pro，与运行同一模型的三星手机之间的差异，远小于 2015 年 iPhone 6 与任何安卓设备之间的差距。

What’s Actually Working

实际上在运转的部分

Services. Apple’s services segment — App Store, iCloud, Apple Pay, subscriptions — is growing at double-digit rates and now runs at a $100 billion+ annual revenue rate with significantly higher margins than hardware. This is the compounding flywheel that doesn’t require Apple to win the AI model race. Every iPhone sold extends the services runway. Apple Intelligence features that improve iPhone retention — even modestly — have a large multiplier on services revenue. That is the honest bull case: not that Apple wins AI, but that AI modestly improves iPhone stickiness, which compounds the services business that is already working.

服务。苹果的服务业务——App Store、iCloud、Apple Pay、订阅——以两位数增速增长，年化收入已超 1000 亿美元，利润率远高于硬件。这是一个不需要苹果在 AI 模型竞争中获胜就能自我复利的飞轮。每卖出一台 iPhone 都延长了服务业务的跑道。Apple Intelligence 功能只要能小幅提升 iPhone 留存率，就会对已经运转中的服务业务产生巨大的倍增效应。这才是诚实的多头论点：不是苹果赢得 AI，而是 AI 小幅提升 iPhone 粘性，进而复利于服务业务。

Morgan Stanley’s survey found 32% of US iPhone users now have an AI-capable device, with adoption “stronger than initially expected” and users willing to pay up to $9/month for Apple Intelligence features. That is a potential premium services tier that does not yet exist. Apple has the installed base and the trust relationship to monetize it. Whether the product is good enough to justify the charge is the open question.

摩根士丹利的调查发现，32% 的美国 iPhone 用户已拥有支持 AI 的设备，采用率”强于初始预期”，用户愿意为 Apple Intelligence 功能每月支付最高约 9 美元。这是一个尚不存在的潜在高端服务层级。苹果有足够的用户基础和信任关系来变现它——产品够不够好到让用户愿意付费，是开放的问题。

The Conclusion

结论

At 32x forward earnings, Apple is the most expensively priced company in the Magnificent Seven for a product that is behind schedule. That is a real risk. The Siri delay is not a bug fix; it reflects something structural about Apple’s difficulty executing on AI when it does not control the model layer.

以 32 倍远期市盈率，苹果是科技七巨头中为一个落后于时间表的产品付出最高定价的公司。风险很具体。Siri 的延迟不是一个需要修补的 bug，它反映了苹果在不掌控模型层时在 AI 执行上的结构性困难。

The commoditization bet is coherent — and it might be right. If foundation models converge toward commodity pricing over the next three to five years, and if privacy regulation tightens in ways that advantage Apple’s architecture, the current capex restraint will look prescient rather than timid. The services flywheel keeps compounding regardless. The bull case doesn’t require Apple to win the AI race. It requires Apple not to lose the device and ecosystem layer while the race plays out. On that narrower question, the answer is not obvious — but it is more defensible than the bear narrative of inevitable obsolescence suggests.

商品化的赌注在逻辑上站得住脚——而且可能是对的。如果基础模型在未来三到五年向商品化定价收敛，如果隐私法规以有利于苹果架构的方式收紧，当前的资本支出克制会显得有先见之明而非胆怯。服务飞轮的复利无论如何都在进行。多头情形不需要苹果赢得 AI 竞赛，只需要苹果在竞赛进行时守住设备和生态层。在这个更窄的问题上，答案并不显而易见——但比”不可避免走向过时”的空头叙事所暗示的，要站得住脚得多。

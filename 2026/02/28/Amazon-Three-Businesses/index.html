Amazon: Three Businesses

发表于 2026-02-28 更新于 2026-03-01 阅读次数：

This is part of a series on the Magnificent Seven. The framing — four distinct scenarios, seven companies that should not be priced as one story — is laid out in the original memo.

本文是科技七巨头系列的一部分。四种截然不同的终局剧本——完整框架见原始备忘录。

Amazon’s market cap is priced as a retailer. Its income statement is not. Understanding the gap between those two things is the Amazon investment question.

亚马逊的市值按零售商定价，但它的损益表不是一家零售商的样子。理解这两者之间的差距，就是亚马逊投资命题的核心。

The Surface View

表面的样子

Amazon reported $716.9 billion in 2025 revenue, up 12%. A company growing 12% on $700 billion in revenue is, by any measure, a large, mature business. The 25–27x forward earnings multiple is consistent with that read: solid, durable, not cheap but not egregiously expensive. That is a reasonable valuation for a sophisticated retailer with a large cloud division.

亚马逊 2025 年营收 7169 亿美元，增长 12%。在 7000 亿美元的基础上增长 12%，无论怎么看都是一家规模庞大、趋于成熟的企业。25—27 倍的远期市盈率与这个判断一致：稳健、耐久，不便宜但也不算离谱贵。这是一个成熟零售商附带一个大型云部门的合理定价。

The problem with that read is that it treats the $716.9 billion as a single revenue pool. It is not. Amazon is three businesses with three completely different margin profiles, three different competitive dynamics, and three different futures. The single-multiple pricing is where the mispricing lives.

问题在于，这种判断把 7169 亿美元当成了一个同质的收入池，而它不是。亚马逊是三门生意：利润率结构截然不同，竞争动态截然不同，未来轨迹截然不同。单一估值倍数一刀切，是定价偏差所在。

AWS: The Accelerating Railroad

AWS：还在加速的铁路

The original Magnificent Seven memo placed Amazon in the “Digital Utility” scenario — stable business, compressing multiple. That framing fits Amazon’s retail operation. It does not fit AWS.

原始备忘录将亚马逊归入”数字公用事业”剧本——业务稳健，估值倍数压缩。这个描述适用于亚马逊的零售业务，不适用于 AWS。

AWS generated $128.7 billion in 2025 revenue, up 20% year-over-year. Q4 alone was $35.6 billion, up 24% — its fastest growth rate in 13 quarters. Operating income was $45.6 billion for the full year at roughly 35% operating margins. The annualized run rate at end of 2025 was $142 billion. A 35% operating margin business growing at 20–24% is not a railroad; it is still a growth platform wearing railroad clothes.

AWS 2025 年全年收入 1287 亿美元，同比增长 20%。第四季度单季 356 亿美元，同比增长 24%——13 个季度以来最快。全年营业利润 456 亿美元，利润率约 35%。2025 年底的年化收入已达 1420 亿美元。35% 利润率、20%—24% 增速的业务，不是铁路——它是一个穿着铁路外衣的成长型平台。

AWS holds roughly 30% of the global cloud infrastructure market. The railroad parallel applies structurally — cloud infrastructure is becoming the regulated substrate of the economy — but the timeline to multiple compression is longer than the original memo implied. The $200 billion capex plan Amazon guided for 2026 (up from $131.8 billion in 2025) means AWS is still in aggressive expansion mode, not harvest mode.

AWS 占据全球云基础设施市场约 30% 的份额。铁路化的结构性逻辑成立——云基础设施正在成为经济的基础管道——但估值倍数压缩的时间线比原始备忘录暗示的要更长。亚马逊为 2026 年指引了 2000 亿美元的资本支出（2025 年为 1318 亿美元），说明 AWS 仍处于积极扩张阶段，而非收割阶段。

Advertising: The Hidden Asset

广告：被遮住的资产

Amazon’s advertising business generated more than $68 billion in 2025 revenue, growing 22% year-over-year. The market treats this as a line item inside a retail company. It should not.

亚马逊广告业务 2025 年全年收入超过 680 亿美元，同比增长 22%。市场把这当作零售公司内部的一个收入条目，不应该这样看。

The structural advantage of Amazon advertising is purchase intent. A buyer searching “running shoes” on Amazon is not browsing — they are ready to spend. Google and Meta compete for earlier stages of the purchase funnel, where intent is diffuse. Amazon’s ad inventory sits at the bottom of the funnel, where intent is explicit. That distinction commands higher conversion rates, which justifies higher CPMs, which produces higher advertiser willingness to pay. Amazon advertising operating margins are estimated at 20%+, and the business is growing at 22% — faster than Google Search (+17%), roughly in line with Meta (+24%, though Meta’s growth is partly TikTok-displacement, a non-structural tailwind Amazon doesn’t benefit from equivalently).

亚马逊广告的结构性优势是购买意图。在亚马逊上搜索”跑鞋”的买家不是在浏览——他们准备好掏钱了。谷歌和 Meta 争夺购买漏斗的更早阶段，那里的意图是模糊的；亚马逊的广告资源在漏斗的最底层，用户已经决定要买了。这个区别带来更高的转化率，支撑更高的 CPM，产生更强的广告主付费意愿。亚马逊广告的营业利润率估计在 20% 以上，增速 22%——快于谷歌搜索（+17%），与 Meta 大体相当（+24%，但 Meta 的部分增长来自 TikTok 出走的预算，这不是亚马逊能同等受益的顺风）。

At $68 billion in annual revenue growing 22%, Amazon advertising is the third-largest digital ad platform in the world. If it were a standalone public company, it would trade at a significant premium to a retailer’s multiple. Inside Amazon’s consolidated P&L, it is invisible.

以 680 亿美元年收入、22% 的增速，亚马逊广告是全球第三大数字广告平台。如果它是一家独立上市公司，会以远高于零售商的倍数交易。现在它藏在亚马逊合并损益表里，几乎透明。

Retail: The Anchor

零售：压舱石

North America retail generated $127.1 billion in Q4 2025 revenue alone. International added $50.7 billion. The margins are thin — 3–5% operating margins in a business that requires constant investment in warehouses, delivery networks, and inventory. Retail is not the profit engine; it is the reason advertising works and the reason AWS has the scale it does.

北美零售业务仅在 2025 年第四季度就产生了 1271 亿美元的收入，国际业务再加 507 亿美元。利润率薄——3%—5% 的营业利润率，还需要持续投入仓储、配送网络和库存。零售不是利润引擎，它是广告业务能够运转的原因，也是 AWS 拥有今天规模的原因。

The interdependency matters. Amazon’s advertising business exists because 200+ million Prime members visit the platform to shop. If the retail experience degrades, the advertising inventory degrades with it. Retail is the loss leader that sustains the margin-rich businesses above it. That is the honest account of why Amazon keeps investing in retail despite its thin margins: cutting retail to improve consolidated margins would cannibalize advertising.

这种相互依赖很重要。亚马逊的广告业务之所以存在，是因为 2 亿多 Prime 会员来平台购物。如果零售体验下降，广告库存随之下降。零售是支撑上方高利润业务的亏损引流品。这才是亚马逊为何在利润率稀薄的情况下持续投入零售的真实逻辑：削减零售来改善合并利润率，只会吃掉广告这块肉。

Trainium and the ASIC Bet

Trainium 与定制芯片赌注

Amazon’s custom silicon program is the most strategically layered of the three cloud hyperscalers. Trainium is AWS’s AI training accelerator; Inferentia is for inference. The third generation — Trainium 3, announced at re:Invent 2025 — runs on a 3nm process, delivering 4.4x the compute performance of Trainium 2 and roughly 4x better energy efficiency. Trainium 4 is already on the roadmap, making this a multi-generation program rather than a one-off experiment.

亚马逊的定制芯片项目是三家超大规模云厂商中战略层次最深的。Trainium 是 AWS 的 AI 训练加速器，Inferentia 用于推理。第三代 Trainium 3 在 re:Invent 2025 上发布，采用 3nm 工艺，计算性能是 Trainium 2 的 4.4 倍，能效提升约 4 倍。Trainium 4 已在路线图上——这不是一次性实验，而是多代演进的项目。

The scale validates the trajectory. Project Rainier — activated in December 2025 — deployed 500,000 Trainium 2 chips in a 1,200-acre Indiana facility to train Anthropic’s Claude. Amazon’s $4 billion investment in Anthropic creates an internal demand signal that is structurally different from selling to third parties: Amazon has locked-in training volume for its own models, and Anthropic’s Claude runs on Trainium infrastructure. This means AWS margins on Anthropic’s compute are not shared with Nvidia — the economics stay inside the ecosystem. As Trainium 3 scales into 2026 and Inferentia handles more inference load, the percentage of AWS AI compute revenue running on AWS’s own silicon grows. The margin implication compounds with revenue.

规模验证了轨迹。Project Rainier——2025 年 12 月激活——在印第安纳州 1200 英亩的设施里部署了 50 万颗 Trainium 2 芯片，用于训练 Anthropic 的 Claude。这个部署的战略意义在于：亚马逊向 Anthropic 投资了 40 亿美元，而 Anthropic 训练 Claude 就是亚马逊的内部需求——不需要向外拉客，训练量已经锁定。这意味着 Anthropic 计算这块利润不用让给英伟达一分，经济价值留在自己体系里。随着 Trainium 3 在 2026 年规模化，Inferentia 承接更多推理负载，AWS AI 计算中跑在自研硅上的比例持续扩大——每扩大一点，毛利率就好看一点。

Alexa+ and the Consumer AI Layer

Alexa+ 与消费端 AI 层

Amazon launched Alexa+ in February 2025 — a complete generative AI rebuild of a voice assistant that had 500+ million device installations but had become a punchline. The strategic logic: if AI assistants become the primary interface for commerce and search, Amazon needs to own the consumer layer, not just the cloud infrastructure layer.

亚马逊于 2025 年 2 月推出 Alexa+——对一个拥有 5 亿多台设备安装量但已沦为笑柄的语音助手进行彻底的生成式 AI 重建。背后的逻辑是：如果 AI 助手成为商业和搜索的主要交互入口，亚马逊不能只拥有云基础设施这一层，它还需要拿住消费端。

The difference from Microsoft’s Copilot or Google’s Gemini: Alexa+ is free for Prime members and positioned as an agent that completes tasks — booking Expedia travel, getting Angi quotes, ordering through Yelp — rather than a productivity tool or a chatbot. The monetization model is implicit: every Alexa+ transaction that completes on Amazon’s platform is either a retail sale or an advertising attribution event. As of February 2026, Alexa+ became available to all US users. The question is whether generative AI actually makes Alexa useful enough to change behavior — the previous Alexa had reach without stickiness. That failure mode is the risk.

与微软 Copilot 或谷歌 Gemini 的区别在于：Alexa+ 对 Prime 会员免费，定位是完成任务的代理——预订 Expedia 旅行、获取 Angi 报价、通过 Yelp 下单——而非生产力工具或聊天机器人。变现模式是隐性的：每一笔通过 Alexa+ 完成的交易，要么是亚马逊平台的零售销售，要么是广告归因事件。2026 年 2 月，Alexa+ 向全体美国用户开放。问题在于生成式 AI 能否让 Alexa 真正变得有用到足以改变行为——上一代 Alexa 有触达没粘性——这种失败模式是悬在上面的问题。

The Conclusion

结论

The mispricing is structural. Amazon’s ~25x forward earnings multiple treats $716.9 billion in revenue as a single business. It is not. AWS alone — $128.7 billion in revenue, $45.6 billion in operating income, 35% margins, growing 24% — would command a significant standalone multiple. Advertising — $68 billion, growing 22%, purchase-intent inventory — is a top-three global ad platform priced as a retail line item. Retail is the anchor that makes both work, not the business being valued.

定价偏差是结构性的。亚马逊约 25 倍的远期市盈率把 7169 亿美元的收入当成一门生意来定价，而它不是。仅 AWS 一项——1287 亿美元收入、456 亿美元营业利润、35% 利润率、24% 增速——独立上市会获得远高于此的估值倍数。广告业务——680 亿美元、22% 增速、漏斗底部的精准购买意图——是全球前三大广告平台，却被定价为一个零售子项目。零售是让这两者都能运转的压舱石，不是被定价的那个业务。

The alpha is in the decomposition. The Magnificent Seven memo’s core insight applies here more than anywhere: the index prices these seven companies as one story. Within Amazon itself, the market is making the same error — pricing three businesses as one.

超额收益来自拆解。科技七巨头备忘录的核心洞察在这里比任何地方都更适用：指数把七家公司当成同一个故事定价。而在亚马逊内部，市场犯了同样的错误——把三门生意当成一个来定价。

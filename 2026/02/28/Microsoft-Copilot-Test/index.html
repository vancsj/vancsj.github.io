Microsoft: The Copilot Test

发表于 2026-02-28 更新于 2026-03-01 阅读次数：

This is part of a series on the Magnificent Seven. The framing — four distinct scenarios, seven companies that should not be priced as one story — is laid out in the original memo.

本文是科技七巨头系列的一部分。四种截然不同的终局剧本——完整框架见原始备忘录。

Azure grew 39% in the most recently reported quarter. AI services on Azure grew 157% year-over-year. Five hundred trillion tokens were processed through Azure’s Foundry APIs in FY2025, up 7x. Ninety percent of Fortune 500 companies are using Microsoft 365 Copilot. These are the numbers Microsoft leads with.

Azure 最新季度增速 39%。Azure 上的 AI 服务同比增长 157%。FY2025 通过 Azure Foundry API 处理了 500 万亿个 token，是前一年的 7 倍。九成财富 500 强企业正在使用微软 365 Copilot。这些是微软主动拿出来讲的数字。

There is one number Microsoft doesn’t lead with: 15 million.

有一个数字微软不主动讲：1500 万。

The Number That Matters

最重要的那个数字

Microsoft 365 Copilot has 15 million paid seats. Microsoft 365 has approximately 440 million commercial users. That’s a 3.4% conversion rate — after roughly 18 months of general availability, aggressive enterprise sales, and a pricing model set at $30 per user per month. Gartner surveyed Copilot enterprise customers and found that only 5% of organizations moved from a pilot to a larger-scale deployment. Seventy percent of Fortune 500 companies “use” Copilot, but for most, that means a department-level pilot, not a company-wide rollout.

微软 365 Copilot 有 1500 万付费席位。微软 365 的商业用户约为 4.4 亿，转化率是 3.4%——这是经过约 18 个月全面上市、密集企业销售、以及每用户每月 30 美元定价之后的结果。Gartner 调查了 Copilot 企业客户，发现只有 5% 的组织将试点扩展到了更大规模的部署。七成财富 500 强”在使用”Copilot，但对大多数公司来说，这意味着部门级试点，不是全公司推广。

The 15 million number matters because it’s where Microsoft’s AI investment meets a pay-as-you-go test. Every Copilot seat is $30/month. If 440 million M365 users converted, that’s $158 billion in annual incremental revenue — roughly Microsoft’s entire current annual revenue. If the 3.4% penetration rate holds, it’s $5.4 billion ARR, meaningful but not transformative. The distance between those two outcomes is the entire Microsoft valuation debate.

1500 万这个数字之所以重要，是它把微软的 AI 投入逼到了市场面前，要接受真刀真枪的考验。每个 Copilot 席位每月 30 美元。如果 4.4 亿 M365 用户全部转化，这是 1580 亿美元的年度增量收入——大约等于微软目前的全年总收入。如果 3.4% 的渗透率维持不变，年化收入约 54 亿美元，说得过去但改变不了大盘。这两个结果之间的距离，就是微软估值争议的全部内容。

The Two Azure Bets

Azure 的两个赌注

Azure is growing at 39% — a number that exceeds analyst estimates and signals genuine demand. Within that 39%, AI services contributed 16 percentage points. The other 23 points came from traditional cloud workload migration and existing enterprise contracts. This matters: Azure’s growth is real and broad-based, not entirely dependent on AI hype cycles. Even if AI spending softens, Azure has a substantial non-AI foundation that continues expanding.

Azure 增速 39%，超出分析师预期——企业在花真金白银买，不是在泡沫里定价。在这 39% 里，AI 服务贡献了 16 个百分点，其余 23 个百分点来自传统云工作负载迁移和现有企业合同。Azure 的底座不单薄：即便 AI 支出降温，那 23 个百分点不会凭空消失。

But those 16 AI percentage points are growing at 157% year-over-year. That’s the component the market is trying to price: structural demand, or the hyperscaler CapEx cycle in disguise? When Microsoft’s customers build AI applications on Azure, they are paying for compute — metered infrastructure, the same model as AWS. It does not require Copilot to succeed. Azure can keep growing at 30-40% even if M365 Copilot stalls at 5% penetration.

但这 16 个 AI 百分点的增速是 157%。这才是市场试图定价的东西：结构性需求，还是超大规模云厂商资本支出周期的变装版？当微软的客户在 Azure 上构建 AI 应用时，他们为计算付费——按量计费的基础设施，和 AWS 是同一个商业模式。这不需要 Copilot 成功。即便 M365 Copilot 渗透率卡在 5%，Azure 也可以继续维持 30-40% 的增速。

Where GitHub Copilot Succeeded

GitHub Copilot 为什么成功了

GitHub Copilot is the counterargument to Copilot skepticism. It has 4.7 million paying subscribers, with Enterprise customers growing 75% sequentially in Q4 FY2025. Developers use it because the feedback loop is immediate: you write a line of code, the model completes it, you keep or reject it. The productivity gain is measurable in the same session it’s deployed. Retention follows naturally.

GitHub Copilot 是个反例。470 万付费用户，Enterprise 客户上季度环比增了 75%——开发者自己选择续订的那种增长，不是 IT 部门强推的。原因不复杂：写一行代码，模型接着补，你当场判断它有没有帮到你。用不着等季度汇报来验证价值，五分钟内见分晓。这是 M365 Copilot 从来没有的东西。

M365 Copilot’s problem is the opposite. Knowledge-worker productivity is diffuse and hard to measure — an analyst who drafts slides faster may produce more slides, but whether that creates value or just shifts the bottleneck is genuinely unclear. The data governance concern compounds this: Copilot surfaces data from across the M365 tenant, which means enterprises need to audit internal data hygiene before deploying at scale. That is an IT project that delays adoption even among willing buyers. Microsoft has had to discount the $30 list price to as low as $15 to move seats — and even at half price, the pilot-to-deployment conversion stays at 5%.

M365 Copilot 的问题恰恰相反。知识工作者的生产力是弥散的，难以量化——一个用 Copilot 更快制作 PPT 的分析师产出了更多幻灯片，但这究竟是价值创造还是只是把瓶颈转移了，说不清楚。数据治理问题让情况更复杂：Copilot 会从 M365 租户全范围调取数据，企业必须先完成内部数据审计才能大规模部署。这是一个 IT 层面的前置工程，即便是有意愿的买家也会被卡住。微软不得不把 30 美元的定价折扣到最低 15 美元来推销席位——即便半价，试点到部署的转化率依然停在 5%。

The Silicon Gap

自研芯片的欠账

Microsoft’s custom silicon program is real but behind. Maia 100 — Microsoft’s first AI accelerator, built on TSMC’s 5nm process — launched in 2024 and runs Microsoft Copilot inference and some Azure OpenAI workloads. The second generation, Maia 200, was announced in January 2026 on TSMC’s 3nm process with 216GB of HBM3E memory and native FP8/FP4 tensor cores. On the CPU side, the Cobalt 200 — ARM Neoverse V3-based, delivering 50% performance improvement over Cobalt 100 — arrives in early 2026. The timeline on Maia shows execution challenges: Reuters documented delays to the Maia ramp in mid-2025, pushing the higher-volume deployment into 2026.

微软不是没有定制芯片项目——但落后了。Maia 100——微软第一款 AI 加速器，采用台积电 5nm 工艺——2024 年上线，运行微软 Copilot 推理以及部分 Azure OpenAI 工作负载。第二代 Maia 200 于 2026 年 1 月宣布，台积电 3nm 工艺，216GB HBM3E 显存，原生 FP8/FP4 张量核心。CPU 方面，基于 ARM Neoverse V3 的 Cobalt 200——比 Cobalt 100 性能提升 50%——将于 2026 年初上线。Maia 的时间线显示出执行层面的挑战：路透社记录了 2025 年中期 Maia 量产的延迟，将更大规模的部署推至 2026 年。

The gap matters for Azure margins. Google’s decade-long TPU program has driven Cloud operating margins from sub-20% to 30%. Microsoft, running most Azure AI workloads on Nvidia H100s and the Blackwell ramp, pays Nvidia’s pricing on the bulk of that AI revenue. Every 16 percentage points of Azure growth that runs on Nvidia hardware is shared margin. Maia 200 at scale could change that — but at current trajectory, it remains a program that is several years behind Google’s TPU maturity and one that still depends on Nvidia for training workloads.

这个差距对 Azure 利润率很重要。谷歌十年的 TPU 项目推动云业务营业利润率从 20% 以下升至 30%。微软在英伟达 H100 和 Blackwell 上运行大部分 Azure AI 工作负载，在这块 AI 收入里要向英伟达交大头。Azure AI 增长的每 16 个百分点里，跑在英伟达硬件上的部分，利润都要让给英伟达一截。规模化的 Maia 200 可以改变这个局面——但按目前的进度，这个项目在成熟度上比谷歌 TPU 晚了数年，训练工作负载上仍然依赖英伟达。

The Railroad in the Background

背景中的铁路

The original memo placed Microsoft in the “Digital Utility” scenario: stable business, compressing multiple. The railroad parallel applies to Office and Windows — essential infrastructure that generates reliable cash flows but no longer earns a growth premium. Office 365 commercial users have grown from around 350 million to 440 million over four years. That is steady, not fast. The multiple on that part of the business is already converging toward utility pricing.

原始备忘录将微软归入”数字公用事业”剧本：业务稳健，估值倍数压缩。铁路化的类比适用于 Office 和 Windows——产生稳定现金流的必要基础设施，但不再享有成长溢价。Office 365 商业用户四年来从约 3.5 亿增至 4.4 亿，稳定但不快。这部分业务的估值倍数已经在向公用事业定价收敛。

Copilot is the defense against that compression. If Microsoft can move the average revenue per Office user from roughly $15/month to $45/month via Copilot adoption, the entire M365 franchise gets re-rated as a growth asset — a $130 billion ARR expansion on existing customers without adding a single new user. The railroad doesn’t get nationalized; it starts charging tolls. That is the bull case. The 3.4% penetration rate after 18 months suggests the market isn’t convinced it happens on any near-term schedule.

Copilot 是对抗这种压缩的防线。如果微软能通过 Copilot 将每位 Office 用户的平均月收入从约 15 美元提升到 45 美元，整个 M365 产品线就会被重新定价为成长资产——在现有客户基础上实现 1300 亿美元的年化收入扩张，无需增加任何新用户。铁路不会被国有化，而是开始收取通行费。这是多头情形。18 个月后仅 3.4% 的渗透率，说明市场并不相信这会在近期时间表内发生。

The Conclusion

结论

Azure is working. The AI infrastructure bet — compute sold to enterprises building AI applications — is generating real revenue, and the non-AI foundation provides resilience if the CapEx cycle softens. AI contributed 16 percentage points of Azure’s 39% growth, but even the other 23 points represent genuine enterprise demand. That part of the thesis holds.

Azure 在运转。AI 基础设施赌注——向构建 AI 应用的企业出售计算资源——收入摆在那里，非 AI 基础层在资本支出周期降温时提供韧性。AI 贡献了 Azure 39% 增速中的 16 个百分点，但另外 23 个百分点来自传统企业迁移，有合同撑着，不是 AI 泡沫带出来的。这部分论点成立。

Copilot is not working — not yet. Fifteen million seats after 18 months is not the adoption curve needed to justify Microsoft’s ~28x forward earnings multiple. GitHub Copilot proves the model can work when feedback loops are tight. M365 shows it struggles when productivity is diffuse and data governance is complex. At 28x forward earnings, the market is pricing Copilot to scale. If penetration stalls at 5-8%, the multiple compresses toward the railroad. If enterprises move from pilots to deployment, the re-rating is substantial. That asymmetry is the investment question — and 18 months in, the answer remains open.

Copilot 还没有运转——至少目前没有。18 个月后 1500 万席位，不是支撑微软约 28 倍远期市盈率所需的采用曲线。GitHub Copilot 证明这条路本身没问题——前提是反馈足够直接，用户当场就能看到它有没有用。M365 的推广则表明，当生产力弥散、数据治理复杂时，它会举步维艰。以 28 倍远期市盈率，市场已经把 Copilot 规模化定进了价格。如果渗透率卡在 5-8%，估值倍数就会向铁路化压缩；如果企业从试点走向全面部署，重新定价幅度将相当可观。这种不对称性是投资命题的核心——18 个月后，还没有定论。
